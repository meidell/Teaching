Why it's hard — and did the wall get it right?

The tension you're playing with

A shared core course can hold 60–70 students, so it only fits the Auditorium — and there's just one. Every year's core competes for it, forcing them into different slots. Push compactness and courses collide on rooms or professors; spread them and students get gaps. The 2.5h red classes make the 14h15 and 16h30 slots expensive, because they bleed into the next slot.

A hand-built wall is usually feasible but not optimal — people stop once nothing clashes. Run Optimise from a feasible board and watch the gap-slots and wasted seats keep falling: that gap is the room a solver buys you. Try dropping rooms in use to 5, or pushing 2.5h classes to 50%, to see it fight.
