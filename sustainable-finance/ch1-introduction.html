Sustainable Investing:
From Margin to Mainstream

A client — or your own treasury committee — puts the question on the table: "Can we put CHF 50 million to work without compromising our values, and without getting burned by a fund that only looks green?" Ten years ago that sat at the margins. Today it lands on the desk of every asset manager, pension trustee and corporate treasurer in Switzerland.

Answering it well means speaking a precise language: what sustainable investing actually is, how big the market really is, and how to tell a credible product from greenwashing. This chapter gives you that shared vocabulary — the foundation the rest of the course, and your written assignment on Apple's green bond, will build on.

By the end of this chapter you will be able to

  • Define sustainable investing and place any strategy on the spectrum of capital, from avoid-harm to impact-first.
  • Distinguish the seven main sustainable-investment strategies and read the headline market figures — including what the 2020→2022 dip really means.
  • Tell a credible sustainable product from greenwashing using concrete signals.
  • Explain the PRI as the field's shared backbone.
