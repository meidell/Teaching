Phase 1 — Proposal & vetting · MS1–MS2 · summer to October
Research process · 01Choice of research topic
0/0

Before anything else: find an applied, international, measurable problem tied to your Minor. The kick-off rule is blunt — "you only get to talk about it if you can measure it." A topic that can't generate primary data is not a topic yet.

AI can / can't

Use it to: brainstorm angles, map a sector, surface adjacent problems. Own yourself: the choice of which problem is worth a year — graders reward "independent thinking through your conclusions," and a generic AI topic shows.

Gate — verify before moving on

State your topic in one sentence and name what you would measure. If you can't name the measurement, you don't have a topic yet — loop back.

Research process · 02Research problem & literature review
0/0

The problem statement answers: what is wrong, missing, or unclear in current knowledge or practice — and why and for whom does it matter? The literature review is the engine that justifies it. It is a critical analysis where articles "talk to one another," not a list.

AI can / can't

Use it to: summarize papers you've actually read, suggest search terms, check your synthesis. Own yourself: never cite a source you haven't opened — fabricated or misread citations are the fastest way to fail "literature supports the framework" and to be caught at the defense. The critical voice must be yours.

Gate — verify before moving on

Can your literature review defend your research question without you adding spoken explanation? If the link is only in your head, the review isn't done.

Scored: theoretical groundingScored: literature supports framework
Research process · 02aProblem presentation — question, objectives, design
0/0

This is where the proposal takes shape: the central question, the objectives that serve it, and the method that can answer it. Everything must align — problem → question → objectives → method. The pre-grading form scores each link separately.

AI can / can't

Use it to: pressure-test alignment ("does this method actually answer this question?"). Own yourself: the methodological judgement. Two of the eight "scope & methods" criteria are purely about fit — methods fitting objectives, data representative of objectives — and a mismatch is obvious to a reader.

Gate — submit the proposal (MS2a)

Assemble the full proposal in the template, references in ISO 690. This is graded and worth 15% of your final mark. Then pitch it to the review panel.

Milestone · MS2bVetting, mentor assignment & approval Admin
0/0

The gate between "an idea" and "an approved project." Students express mentor preferences but do not choose; the department assigns under administrative constraints. Nothing proceeds until the proposal is approved.

Gate — approval required

No data collection begins before the proposal is approved and a mentor is in place. The proposal grade (15%) is locked here.

Phase 2 — Fieldwork & analysis · MS3 · November to April
Research process · 03–04Research design & measurements
0/0

Turn the planned method into concrete instruments. Define exactly what you measure and how, so the data you collect will be robust enough to "bring value to the study" — one of the scored results criteria.

AI can / can't

Use it to: draft and refine question wording, check for leading questions, simulate respondent confusion. Own yourself: the design decisions you'll justify aloud — why this scale, why these themes, why this sample.

Gate — Qualtrics approval (mandatory)

Surveys are built in Qualtrics and cannot be distributed until your mentor approves them — a formal quality-control step. Send for approval and wait for the green light before any data goes out.

Mentor must approve survey in QualtricsInformed consent required
Research process · 05–06Data collection & sample
0/0

Field research is the essential, non-negotiable core of an IBM bachelor thesis. This is the part that is genuinely yours — real respondents, real data points — and the part no model can produce for you.

The honesty line

This step cannot be delegated to AI. Fabricated or "synthetic" respondents violate ethical conduct (a scored criterion) and collapse under examination. Robust, real data is precisely what separates a defensible thesis from a generated one.

Gate — MS3 mid-way meeting

By the mid-way meeting you should have data in hand or clearly in motion. Bring evidence of real collection, not just a plan.

Research process · 07Analysis
0/0

Five separate things get scored here, and they escalate: interpreted coherently → critically analyzed → put in proper context → important results clearly identified. Description is not analysis.

AI can / can't

Use it to: check statistical reasoning, draft chart captions, stress-test your interpretation. Own yourself: the critical judgement and context — graders explicitly reward analysis "put into perspective," which depends on knowing your specific case, not a generic reading.

Gate — verify before writing up

Can you name your three most important findings and what each means for the partner — in one breath, without notes? If not, the analysis isn't finished.

Scored: 5 results & analysis criteria
Phase 3 — Recommendations, writing & defense · MS4 · May to June
Research process · 09Recommendations & actions
0/0

The payoff. Recommendations must answer the research question, be implementable, fit the specific partner, be prioritized, and show your independent thinking. "It has to be useful to somebody — not to the student."

AI can / can't

Use it to: structure an implementation plan, sanity-check feasibility. Own yourself: the leap from data to advice. This is where "independent thinking" is scored — generic best-practice lists read as exactly that.

Gate — verify

Each recommendation traces back to a specific finding and forward to a specific action for the partner. Cut anything that's just general good advice.

Scored: 5 recommendation criteria
Research process · 08Writing & formal submission
0/0

Writing is thinking — you write to sharpen the argument, not just to record it. Formal aspects are scored and can subtract points (poor structure or citations carry negative coefficients). This is the safest place to use AI well: as an editor, not an author.

AI can / can't

Use it to: tighten prose, fix English, check citation formatting, generate summaries of your own text. Own yourself: the argument and every claim. If you can't explain a sentence you submitted, delete it — you'll meet it again in the examination.

Gate — MS4 submission

Submit the dissertation + public abstract by the deadline. Structure, English, exhibits, citations and summaries are all separately scored "formal aspects."

MS4 — submissionNegative marks for poor structure/citationPublic abstract required
Milestone · MS4Oral defense
0/0

The truth test. 60 minutes, in person, on the Battelle campus — 15 minutes presentation, 45 minutes examination. Online is not permitted. This is where ownership of the process is verified directly, and why every gate above asked "can you defend this aloud?"

The honesty line

No tool is in the room. The 45-minute examination is designed to surface any gap between what was written and what you understand. A defensible process — the trail you built step by step — is the only thing that holds up here.

Final gate

Defense weighted within the 85%. Grade by jury consensus (1.0–6.0). Corrections list issued for the archival version.

60 min · in person · Battelle15 min present + 45 min exam
