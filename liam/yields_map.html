Brunt · English wheat yields · c.1770

Nature, or Nurture?

Why were English wheat yields the highest in the world? The old answer was good soil — England’s natural gift. Using village-level data, this paper takes the yield apart factor by factor and finds the textbook wrong. Toggle each cause and watch what actually moved the harvest.

After Brunt, “Nature or Nurture? Explaining English Wheat Yields in the Industrial Revolution, c.1770,” Journal of Economic History 64, 2004 (with its companion, “Weather Shocks and English Wheat Yields,” EEH 57, 2015). Values are an illustrative decomposition matched to the paper’s findings.
