Brunt · the market for manure · 1750–1880

Where There's Muck

“Where there’s muck, there’s brass,” went the Yorkshire saying — and this paper takes it literally. Feeding the soil was once a closed loop of farmyard dung. Then bones, then guano from Peru, then Chilean nitrate and manufactured phosphate turned manure into a global traded commodity. Press play and watch a market grow out of muck.

After Brunt, “Where there’s Muck, there’s Brass: the Market for Fertiliser in the Industrial Revolution,” Economic History Review 60, 2007. Volumes are illustrative, scaled to the paper’s account of new inputs entering the market.
