The Risk

When historians found that England's country banks lent illiquid, undiversified sums to single risky ventures — and went bust doing it — they called them badly run. This paper says they were misread. They weren't bad banks at all. They were the world's first venture capitalists.

After Brunt, “Rediscovering Risk: Country Banks as Venture Capital Firms in the First Industrial Revolution,” Journal of Economic History 66(1), 2006. No public dataset; figures here are a synthetic stand-in calibrated to the paper’s claims.
