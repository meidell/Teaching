Brunt · Irish grain trade · 1840–1914

The Trade
That Broke

Before the Famine, Ireland shipped grain across the Irish Sea to feed industrial Britain. Then the potato failed, a million died, and the trade that had defined the Irish economy buckled. This paper follows that flow from the Famine to the First World War. Press play to watch it surge, collapse, and never quite recover.

After Brunt, “The Irish Grain Trade from the Famine to the First World War,” Economic History Review 57, 2004. The flow volumes are illustrative, scaled to the paper’s account of the trade’s rise and collapse.
