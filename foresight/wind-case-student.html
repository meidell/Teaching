Will the wind
actually blow by 2040?

Europe wants 510 GW of wind by 2030 and a power system that is 83% renewable by 2040. The question is no longer whether the technology works — it is whether Europe can permit, build and connect it fast enough. You are the foresight team advising two players: Ørsted (the pure-play specialist) and TotalEnergies (the integrated major).

The case in 60 seconds

Offshore wind is moving from "does it work?" to "can we execute it?" Turbine prices rose ~40% (2021–24), installation-vessel day-rates more than doubled, and permitting in Germany, Poland and the UK regularly exceeds 7–10 years. The bottleneck is shifting from access to projects toward the ability to deliver them.
